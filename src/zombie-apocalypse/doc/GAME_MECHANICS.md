## üî¢ PROBLEM TYPES

### **Level 1: Percent ‚Üî Decimal**
- Example: "Convert 4% to a decimal" ‚Üí 0.04
- Includes tricky ones: 0.5 ‚Üí 50%, 125% ‚Üí 1.25

### **Level 2: Increase/Decrease**
- Example: "Sales tax 8%. Does total increase or decrease?" ‚Üí increase
- Multiple choice: increase / decrease

### **Level 3: Calculate Amounts**
- Example: "Weapon costs $240. Tax is 7%. How much is the TAX?" ‚Üí $16.80
- **Important:** Asks for the AMOUNT, not total

### **Level 4: Final Costs**
- Example: "Food costs $85. Tax is 6%. What's your TOTAL COST?" ‚Üí $90.10
- **Important:** Asks for TOTAL/FINAL price

### **Level 5: Two-Step**
- Example: "Item $450, marked down 30%, then 8% tax. FINAL COST?" ‚Üí $339.12
- Order matters! Discount THEN tax

### **Level 6: Backwards**
- Example: "After 35% off, costs $390. ORIGINAL price?" ‚Üí $600
- Work backwards from the answer

### **Level 7: The Final Calculation**
- Multi-step epic problem
- Population changes + money + interest + division
- Requires all skills learned

## üí° COMMON STUDENT MISTAKES TO WATCH FOR

1. **Percent to Decimal:**
   - 4% ‚Üí 0.4 ‚ùå (should be 0.04 ‚úì)
   - 0.5 ‚Üí 5% ‚ùå (should be 50% ‚úì)

2. **Question Reading:**
   - Mixing up "discount amount" vs. "sale price"
   - Confusing "tax amount" vs. "total cost"
   - Missing "ORIGINAL price" in backwards problems

3. **Two-Step Order:**
   - Doing tax THEN discount (wrong order)
   - Forgetting to calculate tax on SALE price

4. **Rounding:**
   - $45.7 instead of $45.70
   - Not rounding at all

5. **Calculator Dependency:**
   - Forgetting to convert percent to decimal
   - Losing intermediate values (use notepad!)

## üéì TEACHING TIPS

1. **Pre-Game:**
   - Review percent-decimal conversion
   - Discuss when to ADD vs. SUBTRACT
   - Emphasize reading the question carefully

2. **During Game:**
   - Encourage notepad use for multi-step
   - Remind them: one wrong answer is OK!
   - Watch for time pressure in Level 1

3. **Post-Game:**
   - Celebrate victories!
   - Review common mistakes from deaths
   - Use as formative assessment for re-teaching
